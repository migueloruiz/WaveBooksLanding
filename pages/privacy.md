## 1\. WHAT INFORMATION DO WE COLLECT?

### Personal information you disclose to us

 **_In Short:_** **_We collect personal information that you provide to us._**

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you contact us or when you send us a bug report, feature request, or other message through the Services.

**Sensitive Information.** We do not process sensitive information.

**Application Data.** If you use our application(s), we also may collect the following information if you choose to provide us with access or permission:

  * _Mobile Device Access._ We may request access or permission to certain features from your mobile device, including your mobile device's storage, iCloud, and files, so the app can read the audiobooks you already own. If you wish to change our access or permissions, you may do so in your device's settings.

  * _Diagnostics and usage data._ To keep the app reliable, we automatically collect diagnostic and usage information, including crash reports, error logs, app-launch and performance metrics, the screens you view, and app lifecycle events, together with technical details about your device (such as device model, operating-system version, and a randomly generated installation identifier). This data is collected using self-hosted observability software (Measure) that runs on servers we operate; it is not sent to any third party and is not used to advertise to you.

  * _Bug reports ("Rage Shake")._ Rage Shake is an optional way to report a problem from inside the app, named after the gesture that opens it. When you physically shake your device (or tap "Report a Bug" in Settings), WaveBooks opens a report form and captures a screenshot of the screen you were on. You choose what to send: you write the description of the problem, and you can review, annotate/redact (draw over), or remove the captured screenshot and any additional photos before submitting. Nothing is sent until you tap "Send". We collect only the description and the images you choose to include. Screenshots may show whatever was on your screen, such as the titles in your library, so please review them before submitting.

This information is primarily needed to maintain the security and operation of our application(s) and for troubleshooting.

All personal information that you provide to us must be true, complete, and accurate, and you must notify us of any changes to such personal information.

## 8\. CONTROLS FOR DO-NOT-TRACK FEATURES

 Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems and mobile applications include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage, no uniform technology standard for recognizing and implementing DNT signals has been finalized. As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online. If a standard for online tracking is adopted that we must follow in the future, we will inform you about that practice in a revised version of this Privacy Notice.

California law requires us to let you know how we respond to web browser DNT signals. Because there currently is not an industry or legal standard for recognizing or honoring DNT signals, we do not respond to them at this time.
